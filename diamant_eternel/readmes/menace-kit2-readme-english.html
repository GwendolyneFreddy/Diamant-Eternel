Introduction



Lux et Umbra by Chaos-Draco DeviantArt ©2009-2018 Chaos-Draco		 Menace sur le royaume de Diamant Éternel adds two new paladin kits: the Diamond Knight and the Unicorn Rider.

I strongly recommend that you DO NOT select them when you generate your character in order to benefit from all the elements of the scenario: CHARNAME will be able to choose one of these specializations in the course of the adventure. Moreover, they have been specially designed to fit the universe of the Eternal Diamond Kingdom and match its mythology. Also, remember that their abilities will only be really useful during the campaign and may be a handicap during most of the SoA game.

However, if you absolutely want to play them from the beginning of your game, both provide a special quest in SoA that will be integrated in the final campaign.

At last, those kits may benefit from a special mount (a steed for the Knight and a unicorn for the Rider), whose name you will be able to define in installation process.

Description



Ted Welch DeviantArt ©2015-2017 St0rMiee.		 Unicorn Riders are women who have forsworn the love of mortals to dedicate themselves entirely to the unicorn deity Lurue, thus fostering a close bond with unicorns. They normally come from the ranks of exalted rangers or druids, although there have been paladins and even fighters who have chosen this path. Most of Riders are humans, elves, and half-elves, but halflings, gnomes, and dwarves are not prohibited.

There are two ways for a paladin to become a Rider. The first occurs very rarely: having noticed her bravery, her righteousness and her behavior, Lurue comes to haunt the dreams of the fortunate chosen woman who, on waking up, finds a protective medallion on her heart. The latter will summon forth a unicorn that will serve her as a destrier and will fight at her side as long as she serves the right causes.

But, throughout her adventures, the paladine may also help a unicorn and even save it. Both the paladin and the unicorn may then decide to share their destinies. The ritual takes places in a forest chosen by the Elders and lasts for about a week, during which they get to know each other.

In any case, the relationship between the Rider and the unicorn can not be created if another strong relationship already binds one of them. The Rider must not have a familiar or paladin horse, and if she has had any, she must have been separated from it for at least one season (three months). As well, a unicorn must not have served another Rider, and if it has already done, their separation must be at least one season old. The Rider has a closer emotional bond to her mount than she does with her own family members. Just as she relies on the unicorn, the unicorn relies on her to help keep its mysteries safe.

Since unicorns have devoted their lives to protecting their forests and their intelligent creatures, the Rider retreats to the woods and maintains few contacts with the outside world. Because of the solitary existence she must endure, it is rare (but not unheard of) for a Rider to join ranks with other adventurers, but she may leave her forest sanctuary should she be needed to fight evil. In such event, she prefers the companionship of paladins but can associate with any good characters when necessary. Under no circumstances will one Rider agree to join with evil beings, and she barely tolerates neutral characters, unless they are druids.

All Riders must be chaste and must dedicate themselves wholly both to good and their unicorn companions. Should a Rider ever willingly couple with a mortal, the unicorn leaves her company without hard feelings or regret. At last, it is easy to recognize a Rider with its immaculate white outfits and its long and free hair that quickly turn white or silvery.


PRE-REQUISITES:

  • Only women may become Unicorn Riders.
  • Requires at least 16 Dexterity, 15 Wisdom and 17 Charisma.

ADVANTAGES:

  • May achieve Specialization (two slots) in longswords and shortbows.
  • May achieve Grand Mastery (5 slots) in spears.
  • Like all paladins, she is immune to diseases, may heal with Lay On Hands once per day, cast Detect Evil and Protection From Evil once per day per level.
  • Gains spells to protect her forest against evil foes, which replace most of the usual paladins divine spells.

DISADVANTAGES:

  • May not turn undead.
  • May not use two-handed melee weapons to avoid wounding her bond unicorn in melee combat.
  • May not place any slots in Two-Weapon Style.
  • May not wear any metal armor (except elven chain mails) and equip large shields.
  • Devoting part of her time to deepen the bond she shares with her unicorn, she earns 15% less expérience points.
